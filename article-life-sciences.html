Life Sciences Guide

Essential tools for life sciences students

From lab coats to microscopes and dissection tools. Everything you need for biology, microbiology, and biomedical science courses.

Life sciences students need specialized equipment that goes beyond the standard stationery. Whether you're studying biology, microbiology, biomedical science, or any other life science discipline, having the right tools will make your lab sessions and practicals much easier.

Lab Safety Essentials

Safety comes first in any lab. These are non-negotiable items you'll need from day one.

  • Lab Coat — Choose a comfortable, well-fitting cotton or poly-cotton blend coat that covers your arms fully.
  • Safety Goggles — Protect your eyes from chemical splashes and biological specimens.
  • Latex/Nitrile Gloves — Essential for handling specimens and chemicals. Get a good fit for dexterity.
  • Closed-toe Shoes — Lab safety 101. No open sandals or crocs in the lab.

Lab Equipment & Tools

These are the tools you'll use regularly in practical sessions and lab work.

  • Dissection Kit — Includes scalpels, forceps, scissors, and probes for specimen dissection.
  • Magnifying Glass — For close-up observations when a microscope isn't necessary.
  • Lab Notebook — Record all your observations, procedures, and results in a durable notebook.
  • Marking Pens — Permanent markers for labeling slides, beakers, and samples.
  • Calculator — Essential for calculations in practicals and data analysis.

Stationery & Recording Tools

Good notes and accurate recording are essential in life sciences. These will help you stay organized.

  • Mechanical Pencils — Great for fine writing and drawing diagrams. 0.5mm or 0.7mm.
  • Colored Pencils — For labeling diagrams and creating detailed biological illustrations.
  • Erasers — White erasers for clean corrections, kneaded erasers for precision.
  • Ruler — For drawing diagrams and labeling specimens accurately.
  • File Folders — Keep your practical reports, notes, and handouts organized.

Pro Tip

Buy your lab coat slightly larger than your usual size — it's more comfortable over clothes and allows for easier movement. Also, check your department requirements before buying — some courses specify specific equipment.

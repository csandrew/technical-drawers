Calculator Guide

Which calculator should you buy?

The right calculator depends on your course, exam rules, and budget. Learn which models are trusted by engineering and architecture students.

Most technical courses recommend non-programmable scientific calculators. The Casio FX-991ES Plus is a popular choice because it supports fractions, matrices, statistics, and complex numbers while staying exam-friendly.

What to look for

  • Natural textbook display for easier reading
  • Matrix and vector support for technical problems
  • Statistics and probability modes
  • Long battery life and strong build quality

If you need graphing, confirm with your department first. Many students prefer the FX-991ES Plus for its balance of function and price.
